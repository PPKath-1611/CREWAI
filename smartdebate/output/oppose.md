Premature legislation stifles innovation and economic growth. AI isn't a threat, but a tool—one that boosts productivity, creates new roles, and addresses labor shortages. Laws restricting "overusage" are subjective and hinder businesses competing globally. Existing labor laws adequately address worker rights and safety. Focus should be on *adapting* the workforce through reskilling initiatives, not erecting barriers to progress. Let market forces – and evolving best practices – guide AI integration, not rigid, potentially damaging regulations. Innovation demands flexibility, and overregulation risks falling behind.
